Understanding on SMT and NMT will link with current AI engineering technologies, providing a comprehensive framework for students to transition from traditional statistical methods to modern neural approaches. This tutorial covers the foundational history of machine translation, starting from the iconic Rosetta Stone's role as a parallel corpus and moving through the development of Rule-Based MT (RBMT) and the Statistical Machine Translation (SMT) era. By exploring classic techniques, including IBM word-based models, phrase-based SMT, and the hierarchical phrase-based models (Hiero), students gain a deep understanding of the "noisy channel" paradigm and how statistical alignment principles form the bedrock of machine learning in translation.  

The curriculum further advances into Neural Machine Translation (NMT), highlighting the shift from pipeline-based architectures to end-to-end deep learning models. Students investigate key innovations such as the encoder-decoder RNN architecture, the attention mechanism, and the dominant Transformer model, which together have revolutionized translation fluency and handling of complex morphology. This part of the tutorial emphasizes practical engineering: moving beyond theoretical understanding to implementation using prominent frameworks like OpenNMT and Marian NMT, while addressing specific challenges like low-resource language pairs through BPE subword tokenization and back-translation strategies.  

Finally, the course addresses the current era of large-scale pretrained language models (PLMs) such as mBART, T5, M2M-100, and NLLB-200, which have pushed MT toward near-human performance. By linking these technologies to real-world applications, including support for Myanmar and its ethnic languages, students learn how to leverage transfer learning and fine-tuning to build production-ready systems. All practical implementations, including notebooks for Moses (SMT), OpenNMT, and Marian (NMT), are provided in the associated GitHub repository to ensure students have hands-on experience in building and optimizing their own machine translation engines.
