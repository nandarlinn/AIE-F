**Title:** Beyond Chatbots: Architecture, Reasoning, and the Rise of Agentic AI Systems  

**Abstract:**
Traditional Large Language Models (LLMs) operate as passive text predictors, heavily limited by single-turn prompts and static knowledge boundaries. This lecture explores the industry shift toward "Agentic AI"—compound systems that transform models from passive text generators into autonomous agents capable of independent planning, tool execution, and dynamic self-correction. Synthesizing foundational concepts and leading research from across the industry, this talk dissects the core architecture of an AI agent. We will examine critical reasoning paradigms like Chain-of-Thought (CoT) and the ReAct framework, explore how models utilize APIs to interact with external databases, and discuss multi-agent collaboration structures. Finally, the session addresses the real-world deployment of these systems, highlighting popular developer frameworks, safety considerations, and the future of autonomous workflows. Attendees will gain a cohesive, end-to-end framework for designing, understanding, and evaluating the next generation of autonomous AI systems.  

**Speaker Bio:**
Thura is a Software Engineering undergraduate at King Mongkut’s Institute of Technology Ladkrabang (KMITL), specializing in AI. His work focuses on building robust models for data-scarce languages. Since 2021, he has contributed to Burmese NLP at the Language Understanding Laboratory Myanmar under the supervision of Dr. Ye Kyaw Thu (NECTEC). He is currently a visiting scholar at the National University of Singapore (NUS) Computing WING Lab, supported by the "Summer Research Internship" scholarship under supervision of Prof. Min Yen-KAN.
